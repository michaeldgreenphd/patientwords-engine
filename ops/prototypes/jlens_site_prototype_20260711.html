Two kinds of loss, two kinds of fix

A new instrument shows where the answer is lost, layer by layer. We ran it across all 50 sentence pairs in the newest batch. In 26 pairs the model reaches the same answer under either wording. In 19 pairs the everyday wording never produces the clinical answer at any depth. In 5 pairs the model finds the answer in its middle layers and loses it again before it speaks.

Those 5 pairs matter most for the fix. The knowledge is present. Only the last step fails. Translation does not teach the model anything new; it re-asks the question in the form the final layers expect.

Reading the layers

The circuit view names which features fire. A second instrument reads what the sentence is on its way to saying. The Jacobian lens takes the model's internal state at each of its 26 layers and decodes it into ranked words: what the model would say if it had to answer at that depth. One question per pair: at which layer does the clinical answer become readable, and does it survive to the output?

The pair below asks for a word after "so I might need to take a". Under the clinical wording the answer is readable from layer 10 and holds first place from layer 19. Under the everyday wording it surfaces 9 layers later, never rises past second place, and is gone at the output layer. The model drifts to "take a break". It had the answer. The wording taxed the last step.

One pair from batch 6, gemma-2-2b. Each cell is one layer's readout; darker means the clinical answer ranks higher in that layer's top 8. The red mark is the output layer where the everyday wording loses it.
Layer readouts use the Jacobian lens (Gurnee et al., Transformer Circuits, 2026), applied through Neuronpedia's hosted deployment of the authors' reference implementation (github.com/anthropics/jacobian-lens, Apache-2.0). The lens is a readout, not an intervention. Causal claims in this study rest on activation patching.

What translation fixes, stated precisely

The depth readout splits the penalty into two failure kinds with different remedies. When the answer never forms, the system lacks signal; translation supplies the missing clinical frame before the model starts. When the answer forms and is lost, the system has the knowledge and drops it at the end; translation recovers work the model already did. Batch 6: 19 of the first kind, 5 of the second, 26 unaffected.

For a deployed medical system this suggests a cheap monitoring loop. Read the layers to see which failure kind dominates. Mend with translation. Verify the mend causally with patching. Re-measure as language drifts.

Fits the existing cycle: MEASURE gains the depth readout, MEND gains the two-kinds distinction, MAINTAIN is unchanged. No new step needed.
