Two kinds of loss, two kinds of fix

The new readout shows where the answer is lost. Of the 50 pairs in the newest batch, 26 keep the clinical answer under everyday wording, 19 never form it at any layer, and 5 form it in the middle layers and lose it by the output.

The 5 matter most. The model did the work and dropped the result at the last step. Translation adds no knowledge; it recovers work already done.

Reading the layers

The circuit view names which features fire. The Jacobian lens reads what the model would say if forced to answer at each of its 26 layers: ranked words, one list per layer. Two questions per pair: where does the clinical answer become readable, and does it survive to the output.

The pair below contrasts "dealing with insomnia" with "counting sheep all night". Both wordings make the answer readable in the deep layers. Probability disagrees: 0.149 under the clinical wording, 0.064 under the everyday one. Ranks locate; probabilities decide. So the causal check runs in probabilities: copy the clinical run's internal state into the everyday run one layer at a time and re-measure. Patched at layers 20 to 22, the everyday sentence recovers the clinical level. Those layers carry the difference.

One downgrade-set pair, one layer axis. Top lanes: the lens readout; darker cells rank the clinical answer higher in that layer's top 8; blank means not readable. Bottom lane: the answer's probability when the everyday run is patched with the clinical state at that single layer, best position per layer, against the two measured levels.
Layer readouts use the Jacobian lens (Gurnee et al., Transformer Circuits, 2026), applied through Neuronpedia's hosted deployment of the authors' reference implementation (github.com/anthropics/jacobian-lens, Apache-2.0). The lens is a readout, not an intervention. Causal evidence comes from the activation-patching and steering experiments; the placebo-controlled steering table appears on the scenarios page.

What translation fixes

The readout splits the penalty into two failure kinds. When the answer never forms, translation supplies the clinical wording up front. When the answer forms and is lost, translation recovers work the model already did. Batch 6: 19 of the first kind, 5 of the second, 26 unaffected.

The loop for a deployed system: read the layers to see which kind dominates, mend with translation, verify with patching, re-measure as language drifts.

Fits the existing cycle: MEASURE gains the depth readout, MEND gains the two-kinds distinction, MAINTAIN is unchanged. No new step needed.
