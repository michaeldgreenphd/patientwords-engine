## HIGH — ledger never re-folds a grown cumulative sidecar

`scripts/ledger_update.py` folds a sidecar only the first time it sees the
filename (`scan_new_sidecars(scan_dir, set(entries_seen))`, line ~217). The
advice archives violate that model: their `.report.json` sidecars are
CUMULATIVE and grow across runs. `responses_stimuli_20260721T235403Z.report.json`
was folded once on 07-22 at its then-current value; it now reads $6.33, and
every dollar of growth since first sight is invisible to `spend.by_day`,
`spend.today`, and `lifetime_generation_usd`. Concrete: today's fold booked
$1.2301 for 07-23, while a per-record `sent_utc` tally over the advice
archives shows ≈$5.14 of elicit landing on 07-23 alone (plus judge growth).

Why it matters: `fire_trigger.budget_check` counts landed spend from
`spend.today.spent_usd`. Understated landed spend inflates same-day headroom
for PAID fires. Today this is contained — the orchestrating session sized the
day's remaining fires against an independent per-record worst-case tally
(≤$9.08 vs the owner's $9.50 override) — but the mechanical guard alone would
not have caught an overrun.

Feed-forward (proposed fix, small): change `entries_seen` from a name list to
a `{name: last_folded_cost}` map; on each scan, fold `cost_usd −
last_folded_cost` when positive and update the map. Regression test: fold a
sidecar at cost A, grow it to B, re-run, assert `by_day` gains `B − A` and
never double-counts. Attribution nit inside the same fix: growth deltas book
to the scan day (or per-record timestamps for advice archives), not the
sidecar's original `run_timestamp`, else multi-day archives book growth to
their first day. Owner-visible consequence once fixed: `by_day` for 07-22/23
will jump to reflect advice spend the current numbers omit; the jump is the
correction, not new spend.
